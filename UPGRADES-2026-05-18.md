# Substrate upgrades — derived from the 2026-05-18 consulting pipeline

## What this is

A proposal to add skills + patterns to substrate v1.6 based on a gap analysis of a live consulting pipeline (41 candidates across Tier A/B/C/D; pipeline data is private to the operator). Substrate today is built for an already-engaged client. The gaps below are everything *before* the engagement begins (prospecting, pitching, identifying the company, pricing the engagement) and the *vertical category bridges* for AI-native sub-segments that recur across the pipeline.

## How the gaps were sourced

For each Tier-A and Tier-B opportunity, I inferred the substrate skill(s) the engagement would draw on, then mapped against substrate's 82 wired skills. Where the same need surfaced 3+ times with no skill to hit it, the pattern became a gap candidate. The 5 stubs below are the top of that list by frequency-of-need; the back-half are listed for follow-on rounds.

## The five new skills (stubs shipped today)

### 1. `first-pmm-compression` — founding/first-PMM 30-day playbook

**Frequency:** ~19 opportunities — portfolio companies at founding/first-PMM stage across AI-infra, vertical SaaS, and developer tooling.

**Why substrate is short:** `consulting-poc` runs a 5-day POC; `new-client-onboarding-week-1` runs day 1-5 of paid work. Neither speaks the founding-PMM language: *"I am my own product marketer, I need to stop."* The first PMM has no team to inherit, no playbook to read, no calibration history. The 30-day shape is different in kind.

**Outline:**
- Pre-day-1: founder-voice substrate capture (composes `founder-voice-substrate`), customer-call calendar (composes `frontline-contact`), what-already-shipped audit, the *single-message inheritance problem*.
- Days 1-7: VoC sprint (5 frontline calls), positioning v0 (composes `positioning-forge`), status-quo identification, no asset shipping yet.
- Days 8-14: messaging matrix (composes `messaging-matrix`), one customer-facing artifact gated by `pre-publish-check`, calibration baseline for one 90-day prediction.
- Days 15-21: launch-plan or LP-ship (whichever has higher leverage given the company stage).
- Days 22-30: 90-day roadmap with three calibrated bets, handover doc for the founder (or substrate-handover if Kesava continues).
- **Refusal:** without 3 founder/CEO calls logged + a name + a category, refuse. The first PMM doesn't have permission to invent positioning.

### 2. `ai-native-category-positioning` — positioning skill specialized for the agentic-AI / AI-infra category

**Frequency:** ~15 opportunities — AI-native companies across infra, model, agentic-workflow, vertical-AI, and dev-tool primitives.

**Why substrate is short:** `positioning-forge` runs Dunford+JTBD+Raskin generically. The AI-native category has its own conditioning variables: model-as-substrate, capability decay (capability-pin's quarterly rebaseline), agents-mapped-to-JTBD, eval-as-data-analysis, the "agent-washing" hype-cycle position (Gartner G00842058). Substrate has the patterns (`agent-jtbd-map`, `capability-pin`, `agents-mapped-to-jtbd`) but no composite skill that produces an AI-native positioning artifact.

**Outline:**
- Inputs: company stage, AI primitive (infra / model / agentic-workflow / vertical-AI / dev-tool), category-maturity (mature / emerging / hybrid).
- Composes: `positioning-forge` + `agent-jtbd-map` + `capability-pin` + `status-quo-frame`.
- Vertical conditioning: legal/compliance, cybersec/deepfake detection, data-observability, procurement, creative-AI, connectivity, voice-AI, foundation-model platform.
- **Refusal:** without an evidence-tagged claims register (composes `allowed-claims-register`) that distinguishes verified vs self-reported AI capability, refuse. Agent-washing is procurement-failure.

### 3. `engagement-shape-pricing` — pricing the consulting engagement itself

**Frequency:** universal — applies to every one of the 25 Tier-A/B opportunities.

**Why substrate is short:** `pricing-strategic` prices the client's product. `consulting-poc` declares 3 engagement shapes (embedded / retainer / train-team) but doesn't price them. Kesava is staring at 25 prospects with no anchor structure for *his* offer. Blair Enns / Jonathan Stark / Win Without Pitching apply here but to the operator, not the client.

**Outline:**
- Inputs: prospect stage, engagement shape (Phase-0 audit / 5-day POC / 4-8-week embedded / 3-month retainer / 2-week train-team), Kesava's calibration on similar prior engagements (read from `goals/ledger.md`).
- Anchor structure: three-tier (anchor-high / target / decoy) per Ramanujam, applied to *consulting hours*.
- Switching-feature analysis: what moves a prospect from POC to retainer (the diagnostic; the customer-call corpus; the substrate handover).
- Outputs: per-prospect proposal pricing card (`clients/<prospect>/proposal-pricing.md`), engagement-shape rationale, kill criterion (what makes Kesava walk away).
- **Refusal:** without a recorded reservation price (what Kesava will not go below) and a recorded walk-away criterion, refuse. Pricing the engagement on prospect-pressure produces the consulting trap Stark / Enns name.

### 4. `referral-targeting` — hiring-manager identification + value-add cold outreach

**Frequency:** universal — every Tier-A/B opportunity needs an entry point that isn't the front door application.

**Why substrate is short:** `outbound-sequence-design` produces B2B SaaS outbound sequences for SDRs. The hiring-side outreach is a different shape: hiring-manager-not-recruiter, fewer touches, value-add per the YLP referral framework (Yi Lin Pei, *How to Get a Referral*, Courageous Careers 2024), exit-clause closer, advocate-mapping when the HM is unreachable. Voice-enforce still applies; channel structure is different.

**Outline:**
- Inputs: target company, role title, recruiter post URL (if any), Kesava's positioning angle for that company.
- Step 1: identify the hiring manager (LinkedIn search `Role + Company`, "hiring" badge check, founder/CEO-fallback for first-PMM roles).
- Step 2: advocate-mapping if HM is unreachable (peer in adjacent role, alumni overlap, mutual connection).
- Step 3: compose 2-3 touches — Connection request (≤300 chars, value-add), follow-up message (≤900 chars, exit-clause), optional advocate informational-interview ask.
- Voice composition: `voice-enforce` runs on every touch. Operator-voice rules (`wiki/concepts/operator-voice.md`): no kill-list, no throat-clearing, no em dashes in body, Gary Provost cadence.
- Outputs: `<operator-outreach-dir>/<company>/HM-<date>.md` and `<operator-outreach-dir>/<company>/advocate-<date>.md` (private to operator, not committed to substrate).
- **Refusal:** without the prospect's dossier (`data/research/<slug>.md`) and a verified JD URL, refuse. The Sybill demotion is the precedent — reaction signal alone is not hire-signal.

### 5. `prospect-identification` — public-surface company-resolution from anonymized recruiter signal

**Frequency:** 7+ direct hits — anonymized / stealth-mode prospects, early-stage companies not yet indexed, and YC-batch companies pre-announcement — plus the prospect-bootstrap step of every engagement.

**Why substrate is short:** `bin/substrate-bootstrap-prospect` assumes the prospect is named. The pipeline has unnamed prospects (executive-search firms routinely use placeholder names for client confidentiality). Substrate has no skill for resolving the placeholder before the engagement is possible. The stub pattern is the negative case-study: a dossier that documents the candidate landscape without picking one, per anti-fabrication.

**Outline:**
- Inputs: recruiter post text, recruiter LinkedIn URL, role specifics (title, location, hybrid/remote, comp band), category hints (e.g. "redefining how brands get discovered" → AEO/GEO).
- Process: extract verifiable facts; build candidate landscape (≥3 candidates per signal); rank candidates against constraints (location, stage, funding, role-vacancy); refuse to pick a single candidate if multiple fit equally.
- Output: `clients/<slug>-stub.md` with the candidate landscape, the searches run, the anti-fabrication refusal, and a *next-step* (DM the recruiter, etc.).
- **Refusal:** if no candidate clears the constraint set, output a stub and a recommended-next-step. Never pick a finalist without harder evidence than candidate-overlap.
- Composes with: `referral-targeting` (the next-step is often *contact the recruiter directly*).

## What was NOT proposed (and why)

- **A "fractional CMO" engagement template.** Out of scope. Substrate is a PMM/GTM operating system; fractional CMO is a different operator function.
- **A megacorp Director-PMM compression skill** (Anthropic Claude / Scale / Stripe / Adobe). Substrate's compress-onboarding pitch applies but the engagement shape is in-house, not fractional. Tier-B pipeline addresses these via `director-pmm-onboarding-compression` (proposed for v1.7), not a separate skill.
- **A "win-the-deal" sales playbook for the consulting offer itself.** The 5-day POC + the diagnostic-as-discovery is the offer. Win-the-deal is theatre on top.

## Blockers / decisions — RESOLVED (Kesava 2026-05-18)

All 5 blockers resolved. The skills are wired.

### Resolution #1 — Voice register for `referral-targeting` (RESOLVED)

**Kesava's decision (2026-05-18):** "Formal operator-voice confirmed, BUT pull in traces of personal voice too. More human."

**Implemented as:** `pat_operator-voice-warm` — the formal-with-traces register. Body copy is formal (kill-list applied, no em dashes, Gary Provost cadence, past+future tense). One to two **personal-voice traces** permitted in load-bearing positions only: opener empathy beat, one mid-body hedge/confession, exit clause, P.S./signoff. Trace count cap is 2; voice-enforce hard-fails at 3+. Trace count cap of 0 also fails (pure-formal reads as posture).

A 2026-05-08 outbound DM iteration locked the canonical phrasings: *"That fits cleanly into my reality"*, *"I have about 20 years..."*, *"this is a learning role for me, too"*, *"Comfort kills growth, and this feels like a good time to try again."* — these are the warmth direction.

`con_formal-vs-personal-voice-register` carries the per-destination conditioning. The `referral-targeting` skill defaults to Position A (formal operator-voice-warm) for all hiring-manager DMs; Position B (personal-LinkedIn register) applies only to feed-content destinations.

### Resolution #2 — Pricing model for `engagement-shape-pricing` (RESOLVED — CORRECTED 2026-05-18)

**Kesava's correction (2026-05-18, mid-session):**
> "I want to make at least $100K from a client in a year. And we do not do hourly or retainer models. From my portfolio you should already know we are pitching for pricing based on outcomes."

The earlier 50%-of-benchmark approach was wrong for our model. **The benchmark research is archived for reference at the bottom of this section but is no longer the basis for substrate's pricing logic.** Substrate now mirrors Kesava's published offer (`portfolio.iamkesava.com/consulting-offer`).

**Substrate's enforced pricing model (mirrors the published offer):**

1. **Phase 0 — Free diagnostic (60-90 min).** No invoice. Bootstraps `clients/<prospect>/` from public surfaces; substrate runs four audits (positioning / AEO triangle / status-quo competitor / Tier-A pattern coverage).

2. **Phase 1 — Paid fixed-fee scoping (5 working days).** $15K-$25K per prospect stage. Deliverables: populated 10-layer context model + signed measurement contract + 90-day skill roadmap. Phase 1 is the **gate** — no Phase 2 without a signed measurement contract.

3. **Phase 2 — Outcome contract (floor + variable).** Three published continuation shapes (embedded operator / monthly retainer measurement-tied / train-the-team). The floor is an outcome-independent operator draw, **conditioned on the measurement contract**; substrate refuses to treat it as a standalone retainer. The variable is one of three structures (% of attributable lift / per-unit bonus / Brier-bonus).

**Annual per-client floor: $100,000 USD annualised.** Pro-rated for shorter engagements (6mo = $50K, 90d = $25K, 4w embedded = $7.7K). Substrate refuses with `BELOW-ANNUAL-FLOOR` if the projected total revenue from an engagement falls below this. Override permitted only with logged rationale.

**Hard refusal lines (substrate enforces; not negotiable inside the skill):**

| Refusal code | Trigger | Redirect |
|---|---|---|
| `HOURLY-SHAPE-REQUESTED` | Prospect asks for hourly billing | Phase 0 free diagnostic |
| `PURE-RETAINER-REQUESTED` | Prospect asks for monthly retainer without measurement | Phase 1 measurement-contract scoping |
| `BELOW-ANNUAL-FLOOR` | Projected annualised revenue < $100K | Decline or operator override with rationale |
| `MISSING-MEASUREMENT-CONTRACT` | Phase 2 requested without Phase 1 contract path | Run Phase 1 first |
| `MISSING-VERIFICATION-PATH` | Metric not pullable from source-of-truth at window | Engagement declined |
| `MULTI-REP-REQUESTED` | Prospect asks for subcontracted version | Engagement declined |

**Encoded in:**
- `skills/engagement-shape-pricing/SKILL.md` v0.3 (the wired skill)
- `skills/engagement-shape-pricing/operator-pricing-history.yaml` (refuses + annual_floor_per_client + Phase 1/2 bands + walk_away_criteria; `engagements` empty by design, fills as closes happen)
- `canonical/portfolio-consulting-offer.md` (mirror of `portfolio.iamkesava.com/consulting-offer`, the canonical source the skill enforces against)

**Calibration direction:** the $100K floor moves UPWARD as real closes happen. It does not loosen without explicit operator decision.

**Archived benchmark research (no longer load-bearing; kept for ledger).** The 9 sources surveyed (Invoicebloom, Strategic Pete, SideStackers, Geisheker, SaaS Hero, Algocentric, Growtal, O-CMO) returned a fractional-PMM / fractional-CMO benchmark band of $250-$500/hr, $1,500-$3,500/day, $5K-$22K/mo retainer, $15K-$50K project. These are **not** Kesava's model. Substrate explicitly does not sell time (hourly) and does not sell calendar without outcomes (pure retainer). Kept here only so a future agent can see why the original 50%-floor approach was replaced.

### Resolution #3 — Anti-fabrication on `prospect-identification` (RESOLVED)

**Kesava's decision (2026-05-18):** "HARD-FAIL. Skill refuses to ship a finalist when company can't be positively identified. Document the AI-Rocketship-style stub pattern explicitly."

**Implemented as:** the three-gate positive-identification check in `prospect-identification/SKILL.md`:

1. Founder name confirmed via LinkedIn (accessible profile matching the recruiter signal).
2. Domain confirmed (LP + about-page corroborates).
3. JD URL accessible and matching the recruiter signal.

If ANY gate fails, the skill ships a stub dossier — never a finalist. No "soft version where it's probably Company X." The stub IS the artifact. The skill's "Stub pattern" section documents the canonical shape; both stubs from 2026-05-08 and 2026-05-18 are cited as load-bearing precedents and stay stubs until positively identified.

Soft-fail remains available as an explicit override per `con_hard-fail-vs-soft-fail-on-unidentified`, but the default is hard. `referral-targeting` reads the upstream `anti_fabrication_mode` field and refuses outreach drafting against hard-fail stubs.
