# Consulting offer

This page describes the shape, not the price. The price is per-engagement and is set inside Phase 1, after the measurement contract is locked. If a fixed price was the right move I'd put a number here; the work doesn't fit a number until we agree on the outcome.

## What AI-native consulting actually is

Most consulting in 2026 is the same shape it was in 2016 with a Claude tab open. Slide deck, ops review, recommendation, walk away. Clients are tired. AI made execution cheap; judgment is the residual scarcity.

AI-native consulting is structurally different:

- **Substrate runs the loop.** Drafts, gates, citations, voice enforcement, claim verification, drift detection, AEO triangle, win/loss harvest, freshness windows. The system handles the floor.
- **Humans run alignment and taste.** Which call to make, which story to commit to, what real value means in your room, when to refuse the playbook ask. The ceiling is mine.
- **The deliverable is a system, not a deck.** Your team runs substrate after the engagement closes. The artifacts cite themselves; nothing in the work product is a claim that doesn't trace.
- **Pricing is outcome-based, because outcomes are now measurable.** Substrate's measurement contract pattern (every goal opens with a metric, source, baseline, window, predicted confidence; closes with a Brier score) is the same primitive that decides if I get the variable.

That's why this isn't "I have a system, let's try it." That's why the offer below is the offer below.

## Three phases

### Phase 0 — Free AI-native diagnostic (60–90 minutes)

Before we talk price, you see your business through substrate.

Before the call, I bootstrap a `clients/<your-co>/` from your public surface: landing pages, blog, pricing, careers page, public reviews, AEO citations. Substrate runs four audits:

- **Positioning audit.** What your LP says vs what your reviews say. A drift score. The mismatch you didn't see because you wrote both.
- **AEO triangle status.** Presence (do you appear in target prompts), relevance (right framing when cited), manual-action density (off-domain mention surface). Most teams have never seen this.
- **Status-quo competitor map.** What alternative your buyers chose most. Usually a spreadsheet, an internal build, or no-decision. Often kills more deals than your named competitor.
- **Tier A pattern coverage.** Of substrate's 20 strongest operator-grade patterns (each cited by 3+ operators in the codex corpus), which you already operationalize, which you don't.

The 90-minute call shows you the diagnostic. You decide whether the gaps are worth closing. No pressure, no commit, no slide reveal.

### Phase 1 — Scoping engagement (paid, 5 working days, fixed)

If we both want to keep going, Phase 1 is a five-day scoping engagement that produces three things:

1. **A populated `clients/<your-co>/` substrate.** The 10-layer context model: positioning, ICP, voice-of-customer (a buyer panel of up to ten signal sources, synthesised from 3 live customer calls I book and run with you plus the abcodex pipeline's logged reads, which clear roughly 70 sources daily), competitive (named + status-quo), product knowledge, conversion narrative, brand voice, market context, roadmap, strategy. Cited, freshness-windowed, audit-ready.
2. **A measurement contract.** What metric we're moving, source-of-truth system, current baseline, predicted lift, window, verification path. The contract is the deliverable. We don't move to Phase 2 until it's signed.
3. **A 90-day skill roadmap.** Which of substrate's 37 skills to add to your operating cadence, ranked by leverage for your stage. Each ranked recommendation carries a calibrated prediction (Brier-scorable) about its impact.

Phase 1 is fixed-price because the work is bounded: substrate gives me a known shape, the bottleneck is your inputs, the artifact is the contract that prices Phase 2.

### Phase 2 — Outcome contract (variable, anchored to the metric)

Pricing model: **floor + variable**.

- **Floor.** Covers operator time at a steady draw, agreed-on calendar, an outcome-independent retainer. The floor is what I need to do the work without rationing hours.
- **Variable.** Tied to the locked metric. Three structures, picked per engagement:
  - **% of attributable lift.** Most common. % of incremental revenue, conversions, pipeline, or retention attributable to the engagement, measured against the cited baseline at the cited window. Authority follows accuracy: if the lift doesn't materialize, neither does the variable.
  - **Per-unit bonus.** Specific to discrete outcomes. Per logo retained above churn baseline. Per deal won at competitive displacement. Per new prompt won in AEO citation. Bounded ceiling.
  - **Brier-bonus.** I open the engagement with a calibrated probability that the metric hits target. If I'm right with high confidence, full bonus. If I'm wrong, the floor is the floor. Same primitive substrate uses to score every operator. First engagement runs at an internal rate while the public Brier history is opened; the published track record begins at the first engagement's closure window.

The variable tier and the floor are agreed on inside Phase 1. The contract carries:
- The exact metric, source-system query, baseline timestamp.
- The window: typically 30 / 60 / 90 days post-implementation.
- The verification step: who pulls the source-system data on resolution day, what counts as a tie, what counts as inconclusive.
- The kill switch: triggers under which either party walks (e.g., source-system access withdrawn, baseline shifts mid-engagement, attribution becomes unattributable).

There's no fixed-rate Phase 2. I price the bet, you price the outcome, the contract resolves at the window.

## Why this is the AI-native pricing model

The codex synthesis pattern `economic-turing-test-rev-per-employee` (Mann, Taylor, Cherny, Sherwin Wu) names the shift: as execution becomes cheap, the price of an output collapses toward zero, and the residual value lives in the input layer (which angle to test, which buyer signal to amplify, which narrative to commit to). Outcomes pricing is how you charge for the input layer. Hourly billing is how you charge for the output layer in a world where AI made the output cheap.

I'm not selling you my time. I'm selling you the move that produces the lift, with skin in whether it produced the lift.

## Phase 2 continuation shapes

Three structures. Pick one or none after Phase 2's first window resolves.

| Shape | Cadence | Best when | Pricing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Embedded operator** | 4–8 weeks, daily standup, weekly review | A specific high-leverage move needs hands-on operator depth (re-positioning, launch, replatform) | Floor + variable on the engagement metric |
| **Monthly retainer** | 3-month minimum, one strategic skill per month + measurement loop | Sustained substrate operation, your team learns by running alongside | Monthly floor + per-skill variable |
| **Train-the-team** | 2-week sprint, certify your team's first calibration cycle | Your team will run substrate; you want me to set the calibration baseline | Fixed scoping fee + variable bonus on the team's first 90-day Brier score |

## What I won't do

- **Hourly billing.** The unit of pricing is the outcome, not the hour. If the work needs 60 hours and produces zero lift, neither of us wins. If it needs 4 hours and produces the lift, the price is the lift, not the 4 hours.
- **Retainers without a measurement contract.** "Strategic advisor, $X/month, no scoring" is the contract that lets consultants hide. I won't do it.
- **Multi-rep engagements.** I am the operator. I don't subcontract the work to people who don't share calibration history with me.
- **Engagements that won't sign a verification path.** If the metric can't be pulled from a source-of-truth system after the window, we can't write the contract, and the work doesn't start.
