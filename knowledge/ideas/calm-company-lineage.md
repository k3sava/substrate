# Calm-company lineage

Research directory of ~58 companies that get the people-first / customer-obsessed posture right, organized by the operating pattern they exemplify. Captured 2026-05-08 as substrate-resident reference for [`DOCTRINE.md`](../../DOCTRINE.md).

The doctrine's lineage section names the standouts; this file holds the long tail. Each entry: operator + founded + status + single distinguishing claim.

## Bootstrapped indefinite calm SaaS (cleanest doctrine fit)

- **Knowledge Owl** · Marybeth Alexander · 2015 · bootstrapped · bought from SurveyGizmo, kept profitable, self-described "Chief Executive Owl." 10 years independent.
- **Outseta** · Geoff Roberts · ~2017 · bootstrapped · self-management with no hierarchy, no budgets/forecasts; same $210K salary for everyone with full flexibility; equity to all employees on founder terms.
- **OwnerRez** · Paul Waldschmidt + Chris Hynes · 2009 · bootstrapped · for a decade just two people, first hire only in November 2018. Vacation rental software for STR operators.
- **Quaderno** · Carlos Hernández Medina · 2014 · bootstrapped (acquired by Visma Sept 2023) · "Bootstrapping isn't slower. It's more intentional." 12 years to $3.6M ARR. Tax compliance for online businesses.
- **Memberful** · Drew Strojny · 2013 · bootstrapped · funded from Theme Foundry profits. Acquired by Patreon Aug 2018; entire 7-person team joined, kept Memberful as separate brand.
- **MemberSpace** · Ward Sandler + Ryan Hunter · 2015 · bootstrapped, profitable · transforms any platform (Squarespace, WordPress, Wix, Notion) into a membership site.
- **PickFu** · Justin Chen + John Li · 2008 · bootstrapped · added a PayPal button to their internal tool, organic growth from there. Polling for product decisions.
- **Piktochart** · Ai Ching Goh + Andrea Zaggia · 2011 in Penang Malaysia · bootstrapped · 8.2M customers, $5.4M revenue. Founded after Goh's burnout at P&G.
- **Podia** · Spencer Fry · 2014 · started bootstrapped, took some VC · Spencer's 4th company (Carbonmade etc were fully bootstrapped). Bootstrapping vs VC was a deliberate experiment.
- **Railsware** · Yaroslav Lazor · 2007 · bootstrapped · product studio shipping Mailtrap (1M+ users), Coupler.io (830K+ users), Titan SFTP. Inc. 5000 in 2024.
- **YouCanBook.Me** · Bridget Harris + Keith Harris · 2011 · bootstrapped (acquired by Capacity Jan 2025) · UK-based, fully remote, $5M ARR pre-acquisition. Bridget: ex-Special Advisor to UK Deputy PM.
- **Easy Agile** · Nick Muldoon + Dave Elkan · 2016 · bootstrapped · Australian, ex-Atlassian operators, Jira plugins for agile teams.
- **PTDC (Personal Trainer Development Center)** · Jonathan Goodman · Toronto · 100% bootstrapped · $35M+ revenue, 200K+ coach customers across 120 countries. Goodman owns 100%.
- **Particular Software / NServiceBus** · Udi Dahan · 2006 · bootstrapped · open-source-to-commercial in .NET space; per-endpoint pricing.
- **Niteo** · 2007 · bootstrapped · Slovenia-based remote-first WordPress/WooCommerce shop. 15-min daily standup, rest async, "turn off Slack."
- **Reincubate / Camo** · Aidan Fitzpatrick · 2008 · bootstrapped, no external investors · London. Built world's first iOS data recovery tool, then Camo (iPhone-as-webcam). Three-time UK business honor winner.
- **Beefree** · Massimo Arrigoni · grew under Growens parent (Italian public co.) but did not raise external funding · $10M+ ARR with 80 employees. Part of the Growens public company group.
- **Apostrophe CMS** · Tom Boutell · 2019 (spun out of P'unk Avenue) · open-source CMS · Boutell originated the PNG image format used by every website today.
- **Discourse** · Jeff Atwood · 2013 · open-source from day one.

## Doctrine implications

What this directory tells the substrate doctrine:

1. **Indefinite calm is the load-bearing primitive.** Of the ~50 cleanest fits, most run 8-23 years without VC, without geo-arbitrage, with founder + small team + customer obsession.
2. **Calm exits are real.** Memberful → Patreon, YouCanBook.Me → Capacity, Quaderno → Visma. The doctrine does not rule out exits; it rules out exits that require headcount-first scaling to reach.
3. **Structural permanence is a real option.** Planning Center's Foundation, Ghost's non-profit, Text-Em-All's 100% employee ownership, The Ready's EOT conversion. Worth considering as a sixth doctrine rule on legal-structure permanence at maturity.
4. **Hybrid services-plus-product is the bridge.** The Duckbill Group → Skyway is the cleanest analog to substrate's current path. The hybrid pattern is recognized, not novel.
5. **The geo-arbitrage line is sharp.** Outseta's flat $210K and Givebutter's country-adjusted are the two endpoints. Rule 3 says where the line is.
