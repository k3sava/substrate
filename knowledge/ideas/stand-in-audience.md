# Stand-in audience

A panel of buyer-shaped readers used to score a draft before any human reviews it. The technical name for the same idea is "synthetic audience" — a panel built from real persona data, not made-up readers.

## What it is

A set of personas, each with a role, a state (early research, evaluating, ready to buy), a vertical, and a known set of objections. Each persona is composed from real data: ICP rules, voice-of-customer extracts, sales-call transcripts, public reviews.

When a draft is generated, every persona reads it independently and produces:

- A score from 1 to 5 on **outcomes** ("Does this make me believe I'd get the result I need?")
- A score from 1 to 5 on **differentiation** ("Is it clear why this and not the alternatives?")
- A short note on what worked, what missed, what was unclear

The composite is the average across personas. The notes get attached to the draft for the human reviewer.

## Why it works

Internal applause is not signal. The marketing team has its own taste, which is correlated with the buyer's taste only sometimes. The stand-in audience holds the draft to a buyer-shaped standard, not a teammate-shaped one.

The panel collapses most reviewer cycles. A draft that scores below the floor goes back to the operator before a human reads it. A draft that passes goes to the reviewer with a report that frames what to look at.

## What it isn't

It isn't a replacement for real customer research. The personas are composed from real research; the panel is a fast iteration loop on top. Real customer interviews, real review reads, real call transcripts still feed the personas. The panel is the runtime, not the substrate.

## How to build a panel

1. Start with the project's ICP. List the primary segment plus two or three secondary segments.
2. For each segment, list the buyer-states relevant to the asset (e.g., "first-touch awareness," "actively evaluating," "ready to buy but stuck").
3. For each (segment × state), pull the most recent five voice-of-customer quotes and the most common three objections from sales/CS calls.
4. Compose one persona per (segment × state) cell. Six to twelve cells total is the right size.
5. Calibrate: run the panel against three already-shipped drafts and check the panel's score against actual outcome. Adjust personas if the panel disagrees consistently.

## Where it fits in the loop

Step 5 (checks before publishing) runs the stand-in audience as one of four sub-gates. Drafts below the floor don't reach the human reviewer.
