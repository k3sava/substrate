# Account is the unit of pursuit, not the lead

## Convergence

Four operators across ABM architecture (Sangram Vajre), demand-engineering (Jon Miller), strategic-account programs (Bev Burgess), and ICP design (Maja Voje) converge on the same operating claim: in B2B with multi-buyer committees, the lead-as-unit-of-pursuit framework underweights the committee dynamic that actually decides the deal. The MQL → SQL → Opp funnel optimizes for individual progression; the deal happens at the account level where 6-10 stakeholders converge on a yes-or-no. Switching the unit-of-record from lead to account is not a tooling change; it's a reorientation of how pipeline is built and measured.

## Operators

- **Sangram Vajre** (Terminus, GTM Partners), `ins_vajre-tear-mql-funnel`. Vajre's claim: the MQL funnel is "GTM debt." It rewards lead volume over account quality and creates a dynamic where marketing celebrates lead counts that don't convert because the buying-committee context is missing. Vajre's reframe: TEAM (Target → Engage → Activate → Measure) at the account level, with the leads inside an account treated as signals about that account's buying state, not as standalone units of pipeline.
- **Jon Miller** (Marketo co-founder, Engagio, now Demandbase), `ins_miller-account-as-revenue-unit`. Miller's framework: the account is the revenue unit. The lead is the inroad. ABM measures account-level engagement (multi-buyer touches, account-fit score, account-stage progression), not lead-level MQL counts. Miller's data: ABM accounts close at 2-3x the rate of inbound-only accounts and at higher ACV, but only when the account-level metrics replace, not supplement, the lead-level metrics.
- **Bev Burgess** (ITSMA, founder of strategic-account-marketing practice), `ins_burgess-strategic-account-program`. Burgess's "1-to-1 ABM" doctrine for the highest-tier accounts: a named SDR + named AE + named PMM build a custom plan for a single account, with stakeholder maps, custom content, executive engagement plans. Below that tier, "1-to-few" (cluster of 5-15 accounts with shared trigger or persona) and "1-to-many" (broader programmatic ABM). All three tiers treat the account as the unit; only the depth of pursuit varies.
- **Maja Voje** (Go-to-Market Strategist, GTM Strategist book), `ins_voje-icp-account-shape`. Voje's ICP framework explicitly anchors at the account level: the ICP cell is firmographic + technographic + behavioral. A lead matching the persona in a non-ICP account is lower-value than a lead in the ICP account who doesn't yet match the persona. Voje's measurement: account-fit weight is 60-70% of total lead score in mature B2B; persona-fit is the residual.

## Variation

- **Vajre** names the *funnel rejection*: MQL is GTM debt; tear it down.
- **Miller** names the *unit-of-record change*: account replaces lead as the system's primary key.
- **Burgess** names the *tiered depth model*: 1-to-1, 1-to-few, 1-to-many — same unit, different intensity.
- **Voje** names the *scoring rebalance*: account-fit dominates persona-fit in scoring weight.

A complete account-as-unit practice uses all four: replace MQL targets with account-level activation targets (Vajre), measure on account-level engagement (Miller), tier the depth of pursuit (Burgess), and rebalance scoring to lead with account-fit (Voje).

## Implication

For PMMs, demand-gen leads, and sales leaders:

1. **Reframe the funnel.** Account-fit + account-engagement become the leading metrics. Lead counts become a sub-metric inside an account scorecard.
2. **Build the stakeholder map per account.** Economic buyer, decision-makers (typically 2-3), users (typically 3-5), influencers, blockers. The map determines who gets which message.
3. **Tier the program by depth, not by source.** 1-to-1 for the highest-leverage accounts (top 10-30); 1-to-few for clusters; 1-to-many for the long tail. All three are ABM; the depth differs.
4. **Score the account first, the lead second.** ICP-fit score on the account drives prioritization; persona-fit on the lead determines which message lands.
5. **Measure pipeline at the account level.** "Engaged accounts" replaces "MQLs"; "stage progression on account" replaces "lead-stage progression."

## Counter-evidence

- **Pure PLG / self-serve** motions are lead-led by design; the user is often the buyer, the committee is small, and the account-as-unit framework adds overhead without payoff.
- **Sub-$10K ACV transactional B2B** rarely has a 6-person committee; lead-as-unit is fine.
- **Bottom-up enterprise** (where individual users adopt before sales engages) starts lead-led and only switches to account-led at the expansion / contracting stage.
- **Channel-led / partner-led** motions have a different unit: the partner relationship, not the end-account.
