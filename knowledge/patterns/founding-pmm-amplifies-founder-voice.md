# The first PMM amplifies the founder's clarity, not their own taste

## Convergence

Four operators converge on the same operating rule for the first / founding PMM: the load-bearing artifact is the founder's voice amplified into a system, not a generic positioning canon imported from the operator's prior work. The first PMM has no team to inherit, no customer-call corpus, no calibration history, no canonical positioning to gate against. The only substrate that already exists is the founder's repeated claims. Codifying those, gating against them, and propagating them across surfaces is the job. Imported playbooks fail because the company hasn't earned the language yet.

## Operators

- Anthony Pierri (FletchPMM), `ins_pierri-do-less-marketing-better`. The founding-PMM thesis: fewer surfaces, sharper claim, founder-grounded copy. Pierri's homepage methodology lifts language verbatim from sales calls and founder transcripts before any PMM rewrite.
- Andy Raskin, `ins_raskin-narrative-is-founder-voice`. The strategic narrative is the founder's worldview compressed into a five-act structure. The PMM's craft is amplification, not authoring. Raskin's frame: "the founder already has the new game in their head; your job is to name it."
- A recurring founder pattern (AI-native Series A/B, 2025-26), `ins_shewey-i-am-my-own-pmm`. The founder running their own PMM until the first hire arrives carries the entire substrate in their head. The first PMM's first 30 days is voice-capture and codification; shipping new artifacts before that step inverts the load-bearing relationship.
- Mike Maples Jr., `ins_maples-first-pmm-names-new-game`. Inflection theory: the first PMM at an inflection-stage company is the one who names the new game, but the inflection itself comes from the founder's insight. The PMM ships the artifact; the founder ships the bet.

## Variation

- Pierri operationalises the rule at the *homepage layer* — verbatim founder language as copy substrate.
- Raskin operationalises it at the *strategic narrative layer* — five-act structure with founder-voice as the load-bearing claim.
- The founder-side mirror — the founder who's been doing PMM names the substrate problem.
- Maples extends it to the *category-creation layer* — the new game came from the founder; the PMM names it for the buyer.
- Convergence: the first PMM's first job is voice capture + codification. Artifact shipping comes after.

## Implication

For first / founding PMM engagements:

1. **Before any artifact, capture founder voice as substrate.** Cadence stats, kill-list, reusable narrative beats, voice-anchors. Without this, every asset carries the PMM's voice not the founder's, and the founder will reject the work even when it's craft-correct.
2. **Refuse to ship positioning v0 before founder-voice capture.** The positioning isn't the PMM's authored claim; it's the founder's already-existing claim made legible.
3. **Refuse to import a playbook.** Generic PMM playbooks fail at founding stage because they assume a customer-call corpus and a positioning canon. Neither exists yet. Run the voice-capture + frontline-contact loop instead.
4. **Gate every artifact against the founder's voice substrate.** Voice-enforce isn't just kill-list; it's "would the founder say this on a podcast." If not, re-emit.
5. **Hand over the substrate, not the artifact.** Engagement-end deliverable is the founder running PMM substrate after the engagement closes. Artifacts are the side-effect.

## Counter-evidence

- For founder-heavy product-led companies where the founder has *no PMM instinct* (technical founder building a developer tool, B2C founder building a consumer surface), the founder-voice substrate may be too thin to amplify. In that case, the first PMM's voice gradually becomes the substrate — but only after 60-90 days of frontline-contact loops generate enough customer language to overwrite the operator's defaults. Pierri flags this exception explicitly.
- For category-mature companies (a "first PMM" hire at a Series C with an existing message house), the rule inverts — the PMM is closing voice drift, not amplifying founder voice.
