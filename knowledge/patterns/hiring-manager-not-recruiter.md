# Outreach to the hiring manager outperforms outreach to the recruiter

## Convergence

Three credible operators converge on the same outbound rule for hiring-side outreach (operator pursuing a contract or a role): the hiring manager (HM) has the decision power, the recruiter has only screening power. A referral to the HM is 15× more likely to land an offer than a cold application through the recruiter. The rule extends to consulting pursuit: the founder/CMO is the equivalent of the HM; the talent partner or business-dev intake is the equivalent of the recruiter. Address the decision-maker, not the gatekeeper.

## Operators

- Yi Lin Pei (Courageous Careers), `ins_ylp-hm-first-15x-rule`. *How to Get a Referral* (2024). The 15× rule: referrals are 15 times more likely to land an offer because they bypass the application black-hole and the recruiter screening stack. The HM has decision power; the recruiter has screening power. Search `<role-function> + <company>` on LinkedIn; top result with "hiring" badge is the HM.
- April Dunford, `ins_dunford-decision-power-sits-with-hm`. Positioning interview methodology applied to the operator's own outreach: identify who actually has decision authority on the buyer side, address them, refuse to optimize for the intake function. (The B2B sales parallel: champion vs gatekeeper. PMM operators applying this framework to their own pipeline use the same primitive.)
- Adam Grant (*Give and Take*), `ins_grant-asymmetric-give-first`. Asymmetric exchange: lead with a value-add the recipient didn't ask for, before any ask. The HM is the recipient whose attention is scarce; the value-add (a substrate-derived hook, a Tier-A pattern observation, a competitive analysis insight) is the cost of the read. Recruiters are not the right recipient for value-add because they don't have the decision power to act on it.

## Variation

- YLP operationalises it for *PMM job pursuit* — the 15× rule, the LinkedIn search syntax, the connection-request + post-connect message flow.
- Dunford generalises the *decision-authority frame* — works for any buyer-side outreach, hiring or sales.
- Grant provides the *asymmetric-exchange frame* — why value-add lands and ask-only doesn't.
- Convergence: identify the decision-maker, not the gatekeeper. Lead with value-add, not ask. The recruiter / intake is a fallback when the HM is unreachable, not the default target.

## Implication

For hiring-side outreach and consulting pursuit:

1. **Identify the hiring manager first.** LinkedIn search `<role-function> + <company>`. Director-of-X for a Manager-of-X role; CMO/founder for a Head-of role; founder/CEO for a founding-PMM role.
2. **Check the "hiring" badge.** Top result with the badge enabled is the HM. No badge = HM-by-inference; reach anyway with a softer hook.
3. **Refuse the recruiter as the primary path.** Recruiters get high message volume and have no decision power; the message is filtered as commodity by default. Use the recruiter only when the HM is unreachable AND the post is the only signal.
4. **Lead with the value-add.** Per Grant: the first touch is what the operator brings, not what the operator wants. Touch 1 (connection request, ≤300 chars) is a value-add hook; touch 2 (post-connect, ≤900 chars) is the operator-angle + ask + exit clause.
5. **Fall back to advocate-mapping when HM is unreachable.** Peer in adjacent role, alumni overlap, mutual connection. YLP's framework: an advocate referral inside the company is the second-best path; the recruiter is the third-best, not the second.

## Counter-evidence

- For **very-large-org hiring** (5,000+ FTEs, formal talent-acquisition function with strict no-direct-HM-contact policies), the HM may be unreachable by policy. The advocate-mapping fallback applies — peer in adjacent role, alumni overlap, internal referral via Slack community (YLP names Product Marketing Alliance as a workaround channel).
- For **legitimate retained-search engagements** where the recruiter is the operator-side advocate (e.g., a boutique search firm that uses placeholder names for client confidentiality), the recruiter *becomes* the gatekeeper to the HM. The frame inverts: address the recruiter with value-add to earn the HM intro.
- For **executive-level roles** ($300K+ base, VP+ titles), retained search is often the only path; the HM may legitimately delegate the screening. The HM-first rule still applies *once the recruiter intro happens* — the actual decision conversation must happen with the HM.
