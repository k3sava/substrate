# Company story IS the strategy, name the shift, run the audit

## Convergence
Four operators (three from Andy Raskin's lineage, one from pitch craft) converge on the same claim: the company narrative is not a marketing artifact downstream of strategy, it IS the strategy expressed in compressed form. Name the world-shift in 1-3 words, frame features as obstacles to the new game, audit drift across decks/homepage/release notes monthly.

## Operators
- Andy Raskin, `ins_company-story-is-the-strategy`, `ins_old-game-to-new-game-narrative`, `ins_name-the-shift-concisely`, `ins_obstacles-as-gates-not-problems`. The full Raskin frame.
- April Dunford, `ins_setup-follow-through-pitch`. Sales pitches need a Setup before the Follow-Through; the Setup IS the world-shift.
- Mihika Kapoor, `ins_pain-solution-proof-interleaved-pitch`. Vision pitch interleaves pain/solution/proof per beat, sustained narrative compression.
- Anthony Pierri, `ins_homepage-five-second-trinity`. Use case + alternative + result in 5 seconds is the homepage expression of the shift.

## Variation
- Raskin owns the *narrative architecture*.
- Dunford owns the *pitch architecture*.
- Kapoor owns the *vision-pitch interleaving*.
- Pierri owns the *homepage compression*.
- Convergence: the same compressed shift, expressed at every surface.

## Implication
Run a monthly narrative-drift audit (Raskin) across homepage, top-of-deck, top-of-release-notes, and live sales pitch. If the 1-3 word shift varies across surfaces, fix it before launching anything new. Treat the shift as a versioned artifact owned by PMM/founder, not a slogan owned by Marketing.
