# Research preview, frontier programs, seasons not roadmaps

## Convergence
Three operators converge on a release cadence shift: traditional 6-month roadmaps + GA launches no longer fit AI-product velocity. Brand work as "research preview" to compress timelines; run a frontier cohort in parallel with normal rollout; plan in seasons keyed to secular changes; replace PRDs with weekly metrics + principles.

## Operators
- Cat Wu, `ins_research-preview-trust-cadence` and `ins_prds-replaced-by-metrics-and-principles`. Brand as research preview; replace PRDs with weekly metrics + team-principles doc.
- Aparna Chennapragada, `ins_frontier-program-tiered-rollout`. Frontier cohort: bleeding-edge access in parallel with normal rollout.
- Asha Sharma, `ins_seasons-not-roadmaps`. Plan in seasons keyed to secular changes, not 6-month roadmaps.

## Variation
- Cat Wu owns the *brand framing* and the *artifact shift* (PRD → metrics + principles).
- Chennapragada owns the *cohort structure* (frontier vs normal).
- Sharma owns the *time horizon* (seasons vs roadmaps).
- Convergence: ship faster, signal less commitment, run multiple cadences in parallel.

## Implication
Replace roadmap reviews with seasonal planning + weekly metric readouts. Brand near-GA features as "research preview" to lower trust risk while compressing timeline. Stand up a frontier cohort with explicit early-adopter terms. Replace PRDs with metric goals + principle docs.
