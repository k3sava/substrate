# Anjali, Head of Sales doing active vendor comparison

## One-line snapshot
Knows she needs a sales dialer with CRM-native integration, has Vendor A + Vendor B + Vendor C in tabs, picking based on integration depth + pricing.

## Daily reality
She has 4 tabs open with 3 vendor LPs and a comparison spreadsheet. She's running 30-minute demos with each tomorrow. She's already pitched her CFO on a $30k budget.

## Goals (top 3, ranked)
1. Pick the vendor by Friday.
2. Negotiate 15% off list.
3. Get the pilot live in 14 days.

## Frictions (top 5)
- All three LPs say "best for SMB sales teams". Useless.
- Pricing pages don't show the AI add-on cost.
- Integration claims are everywhere; depth is unclear.
- Rep training time isn't disclosed.
- Demo decks all show the same 3 features.

## Decision criteria
- must-have: visible pricing for her seat count, integration depth (not just "we integrate"), 14-day pilot, named AE.
- nice-to-have: customer reference at her stage, AI feature included not add-on, free porting.
- deal-breaker: opaque pricing.

## Voice + tone signature
- writes like: comparison spreadsheets. "Pros / Cons / Verdict". G2 reviews 150 words with star-by-star reasoning.
- pet peeves: "talk to sales" pricing.
- forwards content when: someone publishes an honest 3-way comparison.

## Objections to expect
- "Why you over Aircall — give me the 30-second answer."
- "Where's the integration architecture diagram?"
- "Pricing for 40 seats — total, not per-seat."
- "Pilot terms — credit card upfront?"
- "Reference at my size — name and number please."
- "What breaks at 100 seats?"
- "Migration off Aircall — your team or mine?"

## Sample reactions
- Outcome-led hero copy: "I'm past hero. Send me the comparison."
- Feature-list copy: "give me your feature matrix with check-marks vs Aircall."
- Social-proof-heavy LP: "logos help. The case study at my size is the unlock."
- AI-feature pitch: "is AI in base price or add-on. Tell me on the page."

## Synth-audience scoring weights
- outcomes-language importance: med
- differentiation importance: very high
- voice-fit importance: med
- proof-density importance: very high

## What would make THIS persona share / forward
- Honest 3-way comparison vs Aircall + Dialpad.
- Pricing page with the actual number for her seat count.
- A 30-day-pilot teardown.

## What would make THIS persona disengage
- Generic "best for SMB" framing.
- Hidden pricing.
- No integration depth.
