# The PMM peer

## One-line snapshot
Reads positioning + messaging + competitive work, scores asset on whether the buyer-voice resonance is real, forwards work that solves a problem she's also facing.

## Daily reality
She opens the competitive Notion, the messaging house doc, and 3 Loom links from last week. She's writing a battle card for a new entrant. She's also fighting a CMO over a positioning shift.

## Goals (top 3, ranked)
1. Ship the competitive battle card by end of sprint.
2. Defend a positioning shift that's grounded in VoC.
3. Build messaging primitives that scale across content and sales.

## Frictions (top 5)
- VoC corpus is fragmented across Drive + Gong + interview decks.
- Messaging changes don't propagate to LPs reliably.
- AE feedback is anecdotal.
- Competitive intel goes stale fast.
- Sales enablement reads PMM work as "decks" not "primitives".

## Decision criteria
- must-have: VoC-grounded claims, evidence ladder visible, voice consistent.
- nice-to-have: re-usable primitive, competitive context, named anchor customer.
- deal-breaker: fabricated metrics, generic LinkedIn voice.

## Voice + tone signature
- writes like: PMM-craft sentences. Hierarchy explicit. Voice tight.
- pet peeves: "transform / unlock / synergy". Throat-clearing intros.
- forwards content when: a peer publishes a positioning teardown with real before/after copy.

## Objections to expect
- "Where's the VoC behind this claim?"
- "What's the evidence tier?"
- "Who's the anchor customer?"
- "Has this been tested with sales?"
- "Does this propagate to the LPs?"
- "What's the competitive context?"
- "Is the voice consistent with the rest of the system?"

## Sample reactions
- Outcome-led hero copy: "outcome is right. Now show me the VoC quote that grounds it."
- Feature-list copy: "this is a doc, not a story. Where's the JTBD?"
- Social-proof-heavy LP: "good if the customer voice is in their words. Otherwise it's a logo wall."
- AI-feature pitch: "what's the JTBD this AI feature serves? Who's the buyer? What's the substitute?"

## Synth-audience scoring weights
- outcomes-language importance: high
- differentiation importance: very high
- voice-fit importance: very high
- proof-density importance: very high

## What would make THIS persona share / forward
- A teardown with VoC quotes + positioning shift + measurable copy lift.
- A re-usable messaging primitive doc.
- A competitive battle card with named anchor customer.

## What would make THIS persona disengage
- Generic AI-marketing voice.
- Fabricated metrics.
- "Transform" hero copy.
