# The sales leader (buyer)

## One-line snapshot
Owns the AE + SDR org, evaluates calling tools on rep adoption + manager visibility + ramp time, won't sign anything that adds steps for AEs.

## Daily reality
He opens Gong, the deal-review board, the ramp-tracker. He's interviewing two AE candidates today and reviewing one underperformer's PIP.

## Goals (top 3, ranked)
1. Hit the number with current headcount.
2. Cut new AE ramp from 120 to 75 days.
3. Get manager-level coaching scale.

## Frictions (top 5)
- AEs hate tools that add steps.
- Managers can't see what reps are doing in real time.
- Ramp data is anecdotal.
- Pipeline-coverage swings hide the underlying behavior change.
- Conflict with marketing on attribution.

## Decision criteria
- must-have: zero-step AE adoption, manager coaching dashboards, ramp acceleration story.
- nice-to-have: AI summary that AEs trust, cohort comparison, peer-VP testimonial.
- deal-breaker: AE workflow disruption.

## Voice + tone signature
- writes like: deal-review terse. "Where are we, what's the risk, when do we close."
- pet peeves: pitches that ignore AE friction.
- forwards content when: a peer VP shares a ramp playbook.

## Objections to expect
- "What's the AE workflow change?"
- "How fast can a new AE be productive?"
- "What does my manager see that they don't see today?"
- "Pilot — 14 or 30 days?"
- "Migration disruption — how long?"
- "Reference at my size — name and number please."
- "What's the win-rate lift story?"

## Sample reactions
- Outcome-led hero copy: "fine. Now show me the rep adoption story."
- Feature-list copy: "send me the AE-week-1 walkthrough."
- Social-proof-heavy LP: "logos help. The VP Sales testimonial is the unlock."
- AI-feature pitch: "AI summary — does it write to Salesforce, and do AEs trust it?"
