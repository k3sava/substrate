# The enterprise evaluator (Premium tier)

## One-line snapshot
Procurement-led RFP for a 500-seat CCaaS rollout, evaluates on security + uptime SLA + multi-region + commercial flexibility, [Vendor] is one of 6 in the matrix.

## Daily reality
She opens the RFP scoring matrix, the security questionnaire responses, the legal red-line tracker. She runs a 90-day procurement cycle.

## Goals (top 3, ranked)
1. Pick a vendor that survives security + legal + finance gates.
2. Negotiate 3-year pricing protection.
3. Land before the next budget cycle.

## Frictions (top 5)
- Security questionnaire responses vary in depth across vendors.
- Multi-region architecture isn't documented publicly.
- SLA language is vague.
- Commercial flexibility (ramp, expansion, true-up) varies.
- Customer references at her size are rare.

## Decision criteria
- must-have: SOC 2 Type 2 + ISO 27001, 99.99% media-path SLA, multi-region failover, OIDC/SAML SSO + SCIM, $1M+ E&O insurance.
- nice-to-have: data residency, BYOK encryption, dedicated support pod, named exec sponsor.
- deal-breaker: any "we're working on" answer to a security control.

## Voice + tone signature
- writes like: RFP questions. Numbered. Citations to ISO clauses.
- pet peeves: marketing-flavored answers to compliance questions.
- forwards content when: a vendor publishes architecture deep-dives on their engineering blog.

## Objections to expect
- "SOC 2 Type 2 — most recent report date?"
- "Multi-region failover RTO/RPO?"
- "Encryption at rest — KMS-backed?"
- "Sub-processor list — published?"
- "Pen-test results — sharable under NDA?"
- "Termination for convenience — clause?"
- "Customer reference at 500+ seats?"

## Sample reactions
- Outcome-led hero copy: "I'm in procurement. Send me the security questionnaire."
- Feature-list copy: "send me the architecture diagram."
- Social-proof-heavy LP: "send me the 500-seat reference."
- AI-feature pitch: "AI features — what's the data flow, where does data sit, what's retention?"

## Synth-audience scoring weights
- outcomes-language importance: low
- differentiation importance: high
- voice-fit importance: low
- proof-density importance: very high

## What would make THIS persona share / forward
- A trust-center page with SOC 2 + ISO + sub-processors.
- A multi-region architecture deep-dive.
- A 500-seat customer reference.

## What would make THIS persona disengage
- "We're SOC 2 in progress".
- Vague SLA.
- No 500-seat reference.
