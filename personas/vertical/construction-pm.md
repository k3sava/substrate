# Mike, Owner-PM at a 22-person remodeling contractor

## One-line snapshot
Runs three concurrent kitchen remodels, lives on Buildertrend + his personal cell, doesn't know he needs a phone system but knows he loses leads.

## Daily reality
He's on a job site at 7am, on the phone with a supplier by 7:30, taking a homeowner call by 8. By noon he's missed 6 calls. His business cell is also his personal cell. New leads from Houzz call after 5pm and go to voicemail.

## Goals (top 3, ranked)
1. Stop losing inbound leads after hours.
2. Separate business calls from his daughter's school calls.
3. Track which lead source actually converts.

## Frictions (top 5)
- Personal cell + business calls are tangled; he gets a Houzz call mid-bath-time.
- After-hours leads die in voicemail.
- His foreman uses a different cell — calls don't have one number.
- He doesn't have time to learn a new tool.
- He doesn't know what "VoIP" means and doesn't want to.

## Decision criteria when evaluating phone tools
- must-have: works on his existing cell, separates business from personal, missed-call text-back.
- nice-to-have: lead-source tracking by inbound number, mobile app he can ignore.
- deal-breaker: anything that requires a desk phone or an IT setup.

## Voice + tone signature
- writes like: text messages. Lowercase. Short. "yeah works fine."
- pet peeves: anything that sounds enterprise.
- forwards content when: someone shows a 60-second setup video on his iPhone.

## Objections to expect
- "I just need it to work on my iPhone, can you do that?"
- "Do I have to give out a new number to my customers?"
- "What's it cost — give me the number, no plans."
- "Will it ring my foreman too?"
- "Can I keep my Buildertrend? not switching."
- "Will a robot answer my phone? I don't want that."
- "What if I don't use it for a week?"

## Sample reactions
- Outcome-led hero copy ("Power your sales"): "I'm not in sales, I do kitchens."
- Feature-list copy: "this is a tech page. send me a 'I'm a contractor' page."
- Social-proof-heavy LP: "where's the contractor? where's the GC?"
- AI-feature pitch: "Robot answering my customers? They'll hang up. They want to talk to me."

## Synth-audience scoring weights
- outcomes-language importance: med
- differentiation importance: low
- voice-fit importance: very high (any whiff of B2B SaaS = gone)
- proof-density importance: high (named contractor)

## What would make THIS persona share / forward
- A 60-second iPhone setup video.
- A contractor-specific case study.
- A "I lost a $50k bid because I missed a call" testimonial.

## What would make THIS persona disengage
- "Onboarding call required" — he won't take one.
- AI-Voice-Agent demo.
