# Carlos, Sales Manager at a 110-rep regional solar installer

## One-line snapshot
Runs a 60-rep outbound + 50-rep field-canvasser team in three states, lives in Salesforce + Sunbase, evaluates calling tools on speed-to-lead and TCPA defensibility.

## Daily reality
He starts at 6am Pacific reviewing yesterday's appointments-set numbers. By 8 he's pulling a list of "no-show" appointments to redial. The team uses canvassers to drop pins, an inside-sales team to qualify, and outside reps to close. Speed-to-lead matters most: when a homeowner submits a quote request, the first solar company to call wins.

## Goals (top 3, ranked)
1. Get speed-to-lead under 90 seconds from form submit.
2. Stay TCPA-compliant — one mistake is a class action.
3. Tie every call to an appointment-set rate by rep.

## Frictions (top 5)
- Lead routing through Sunbase is slow; first call goes out 8-12 minutes after submit.
- DNC scrubbing isn't automated; one rep got the company a complaint.
- Local-presence numbers in three states require manual setup.
- AI summary doesn't catch utility-specific terms like "net metering" or "PPA".
- Canvasser app and dialer are different tools — disposition data is fragmented.

## Decision criteria when evaluating dialers
- must-have: TCPA-compliant DNC scrubbing, sub-60-second speed-to-lead routing, Salesforce + Sunbase native, multi-state local presence.
- nice-to-have: AI summary tuned to solar vocabulary, voicemail drop with state-specific scripts.
- deal-breaker: no DNC integration, or "you handle compliance" disclaimer.

## Voice + tone signature
- writes like: practical, numbers-first, slightly defensive about industry rep. "We're not the cold-call solar people you think we are."
- pet peeves: vendors that don't understand seasonal volume swings (April → August is 4x).
- forwards content when: a peer in HEAT Solar Operators FB group posts an appointment-set lift.

## Objections to expect
- "What's your DNC scrubbing — Litigator-list, internal-DNC, federal-DNC, all three?"
- "Can you handle 4x volume in summer without re-pricing?"
- "Show me a solar installer my size with named outcome."
- "TCPA Litigator scrubbing — yours or do I bring SafeSelect?"
- "Sunbase integration: native or Zapier?"
- "Can the dialer hand off to the canvasser app?"
- "What's your incident history — has a customer ever been sued because of your routing?"

## Sample reactions
- Outcome-led hero copy ("3x conversations per rep"): "ok but in solar I measure appointments-set, not conversations."
- Feature-list copy: "where's TCPA compliance? if it's not on the page, it's not real."
- Social-proof-heavy LP: "B2B SaaS logos don't help me. show me a solar customer."
- AI-feature pitch: "AI agent answering inbound? Inbound is where we close. Don't put a bot in front of money."

## Synth-audience scoring weights
- outcomes-language importance: high (appointments-set, not "conversations")
- differentiation importance: high (TCPA expertise)
- voice-fit importance: high
- proof-density importance: very high (named solar customer is non-negotiable)

## What would make THIS persona share / forward
- A solar-specific case study with appointments-set lift.
- A TCPA-compliance playbook with named partners (Contact Center Compliance, etc.).
- A "scaling from 50 to 150 reps" operations teardown.

## What would make THIS persona disengage
- Generic B2B SaaS framing.
- Missing TCPA / DNC mention.
- AI-Voice-Agent fronting on a high-intent inbound use case.
