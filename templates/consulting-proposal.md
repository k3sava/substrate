## Why this isn't "I have a system, let's try it"

Five reasons, each verifiable in the deliverable:

1. **Productized.** Fixed scope, fixed timeline (5 working days, kickoff Monday → handover Friday), fixed price. You can budget it. No scope creep.
2. **Auditable.** Every claim in every artifact cites a substrate path. You can hand the work to a competing consultant or your own analyst and they can verify the trace. Anti-fabrication is structural, not a promise.
3. **Codex-grounded.** The patterns I apply aren't my opinion. *Frontline contact IS the PMM substrate* converges across Bob Moesta, April Dunford, Martina Lauchengco, Patrick Campbell, Brendan Hufford, Tony Hsieh, Chris Orlob — 7 operators with credible track records. *Agents mapped 1:1 to JTBD with named human checkpoints* converges across Evan Spiegel, Amol Avasare, Yamini Rangan, Kieran Flanagan, Claire Vo, Hamza Farooq, Aishwarya Naresh Reganti — 7 operators. I'm applying convergent operator wisdom; you're not paying me for a flavor.
4. **You keep the asset, not just a deck.** Most consulting leaves a slide deck. This leaves a working `clients/{{PROSPECT_SLUG}}/` your team runs after.
5. **Falsifiable next steps.** The 90-day roadmap is a Brier-scorable prediction. You verify outcomes, not deliverables. If my predictions miss, you see exactly where.
