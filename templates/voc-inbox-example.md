# 2026-04-12 customer interview, Acme Logistics

[00:00:01] INTERVIEWER: Tell me about the moment you decided to look for a new tool.

[00:00:14] SAM: We had a Friday afternoon where the dispatcher couldn't find a single load assignment in the spreadsheet, and we lost a customer over it. I went home that night and our team needed something that could not lose data again. I was trying to figure out how other 200-person logistics companies were handling this.

[00:01:08] INTERVIEWER: Walk me through what you considered.

[00:01:20] SAM: We looked at three options. We were using Excel before, and we considered building something in-house, but our engineering team is two people and they are heads down on the customer-facing app. We evaluated CarrierConnect and we evaluated DispatchFlow. Honestly we almost decided to do nothing and just buy more dispatcher hours.

[00:03:45] INTERVIEWER: What made you decide?

[00:04:02] SAM: The thing that mattered most was that we needed something that integrates with our TMS without our engineering team writing a custom connector. We needed it within 30 days, the integration had to work day-one, and the price had to make sense for a 240-person logistics company that runs on tight margins. The competitor we considered was complicated, the API documentation was confusing, and I didn't want to ship a project that took six months to figure out.

[00:06:11] INTERVIEWER: What's working now?

[00:06:25] SAM: We saved us about 12 hours a week on dispatch reconciliation. The dispatcher doesn't have to re-key load data into the spreadsheet anymore. We didn't have to hire a third dispatcher, which we were about to do. The peace of mind is the part I didn't expect — I sleep at night now because I know nothing is falling out of the system.

[00:08:30] INTERVIEWER: What worries you?

[00:08:45] SAM: I'm worried about scale. We're growing fast and I don't know if this will hold up at 500 trucks. I'd also love better integration with our customer portal, our customers keep asking for visibility and we have to send them screenshots from the dispatcher view.

[00:11:00] INTERVIEWER: Tell me about the price conversation internally.

[00:11:15] SAM: It was expensive for us, the budget was tight. I had to make the case to our CFO that this would pay back in dispatcher hours. The cost was the biggest objection, but once I showed her the time savings it made sense.

[00:14:22] INTERVIEWER: What would you tell another VP of Operations?

[00:14:35] SAM: That this is the kind of tool you don't realize you need until you have a Friday afternoon disaster. Don't wait. The status quo of Excel and spreadsheets is the real cost.

[00:17:50] SAM: Also, the integration was easier than I thought. The DispatchFlow team had told us their API was complicated. This one was straightforward.

[00:25:00] INTERVIEWER: Anything else?

[00:25:10] SAM: I wish there was a way to see the full audit log of who changed what. We had a load assignment changed last week and we couldn't figure out who did it. That would help our peace of mind.

[00:38:00] INTERVIEWER: Thanks Sam, this was incredibly useful.

[00:38:05] SAM: Yeah, happy to help.
